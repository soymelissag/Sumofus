Influence

According to the 2010 Census counted more than 120,000 Nicaraguans in Miami-Dade County. Due to the Sandinista takeover in 1979, a lot of Nicaraguans uprooted and migrated to Miami. Though the initial move was not easy, Nicaraguans preserves and remain one of the largest immigrant groups.

Culture

Sweetwater is often referred to as Little Nicaragua. Due to the affordable rent, early immigrants were able to adjust to American life.

Nicaraguan Heritage

There’s no denying the unique twist of Nicaraguan culture. In Miami, quinceñara pictures to cafecito culture is alive and well.

Food

From rice and beans to elaborately wrapped meals, Nicaraguan food is a Miami staple.
